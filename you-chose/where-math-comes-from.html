I read this book for fun during a golf tournament weekend one summer. Another parent saw me and said, "Reading a math textbook? You're going to be rich one day!"

But I don't want to chase riches. I want to chase truth and logic and purity.

I chose this book. So I chose to study math.

This was many years ago. Now, I still study math. But I'm no ordinary mathematician. I am the Best Mathematician.

I spent my days analyzing the abstract world. I even got a theorem named after me. History will remember me.

The human mind invented math. I invented my life.

I lived for myself, and I became the best. Now what? Uh oh.

Maybe I should have chosen The Little Prince and thought about actual matters of consequence.
